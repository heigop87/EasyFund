Frequently Asked

Answers to the questions buyers ask most.

How does a yacht loan work?
A yacht loan is a secured installment loan. The vessel itself serves as collateral, similar to how a home serves as collateral for a mortgage. Terms typically range from 10 to 20 years. Down payments are usually 10 to 30 percent of the purchase price, depending on the boat age, loan size, and borrower credit profile. Most yacht loans in the EasyFund network carry fixed rates for the full term, so the monthly payment does not change.
What credit score is needed for a yacht loan?
Lenders in the EasyFund network require a minimum credit score of 680. Scores above 720 typically receive the best available rates. Applications with scores under 680 will likely not qualify at this time. If you are unsure of your score, the soft credit check during the application will confirm it without affecting your credit.
Will checking my rate affect my credit score?
No. EasyFund uses a soft credit pull to match you with lenders. A soft pull does not affect your credit score and is not reported to creditors. A hard inquiry, which can affect your score by a small amount, is only performed after you select a lender and proceed with the full application.
Can I finance a used yacht?
Yes. Most lenders in the EasyFund network finance used yachts up to 20 years of age, depending on vessel type, condition, and marine survey results. Boats older than 20 years may not qualify for financing under standard lender policies. Newer vessels typically qualify for longer terms and lower down payments.
What documents are needed for a yacht loan?
Most lenders in the EasyFund network require two years of personal tax returns, recent asset statements, a completed loan application, and details of the vessel being purchased. A marine survey is typically required for boats over five years old. Documents are uploaded securely through the EasyFund portal.
Can you finance a foreign-built or foreign-flagged yacht?
Yes. EasyFund can accommodate financing for foreign-built vessels and, in select cases, foreign nationals, depending on the vessel location, intended use, and specific lender criteria. Cross-border financing carries additional documentation requirements such as proof of US flagging intent, vessel importation records, and verification of the buyer's residency status. Start with the application and an EasyFund specialist will walk you through the specifics for your situation.
Which lenders are in the EasyFund network?
The EasyFund network includes Wells Fargo, Fifth Third Bank, Shore Premier Financing, Centennial Bank, Octane, and AKF Bank in Germany, along with additional partners added regularly. Each lender has its own underwriting criteria, rate sheet, and preferred vessel types. EasyFund matches your application with the lenders whose programs fit your profile.
